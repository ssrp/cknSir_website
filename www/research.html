The objective of my research is to scale activity recognition in smart home technologies to work in complex real-world settings. This has led me to study activity recognition on streaming data, discovering frequently occuring sensor patterns, and the generalizability of the learned models across different settings. You can find more about my research through the publications.

 

I was the co-chair for the AAAI 2012 Fall Symposium on Artificial Intelligence for Gerontechnology

 

As part of my doctoral work, I developed a computational framework for wearable accelerometer based activity and gesture recognition. Following are some broad research areas that interest me.

 

Theory:

  • Pattern Recognition
  • Machine Learning
  • Multimedia
  • Human Activity Recognition

 

Application:

  • Pervasive Healthcare Technologies
  • Assistive Technology
  • Gerontechnology
